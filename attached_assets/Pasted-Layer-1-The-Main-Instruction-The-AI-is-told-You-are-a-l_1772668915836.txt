Layer 1: The Main Instruction

The AI is told:

"You are a license agreement analysis expert. Analyze this document and extract structured, machine-readable rules."

It's given a list of rule types to look for, such as:

Percentage — things like "5% of net sales"
Tiered — different rates at different sales volumes (like your rebate tiers)
Minimum Guarantees — "minimum payment of $X per year"
Caps and Limits — "maximum fee of $Y per quarter"
Fixed Fees — "annual license fee of $Z"
Deductions — "less 3% for marketing expenses"
Late Payment Penalty — interest charges
Conditions — compliance rules, obligations, restrictions
Data Reference — informational clauses (definitions, territory lists)

Layer 2: The JSON Template

The AI is told to output every rule in a specific structure:

Rule Name: "Volume-Based Rebate Tier Schedule"
Rule Type: "rebate_tiered"
Description: "Plain English explanation of what this clause says"
Conditions:
  - Product Categories: ["Headphones", "Soundbars"]
  - Territories: ["United States", "Canada"]
  - Time Period: "quarterly"
Calculation:
  - Tiers: [{min: 0, max: 1000, rate: 2%}, {min: 1001, max: 5000, rate: 5%}, ...]
Source:
  - Page number, section reference, and the actual text from the contract
Confidence: 0.95 (how sure the AI is)
Layer 3: Contract-Type-Specific Instructions


The system adjusts the prompt based on what kind of contract it's analyzing. For example:

Electronics/Patent contracts → look for "per unit" pricing, ASP percentages, premium multipliers
Manufacturing/Tech → look for Net Sales %, dollar-based thresholds
Plant/Nursery → look for container size pricing, per-plant rates
Distributor agreements (like your Super Lenses contract) → look for rebate tiers, MAP pricing, compliance terms
How Clause Categories Get Assigned

The AI assigns each rule a clause category based on what section of the contract it came from:

pricing → pricing sections, MAP policies, rate structures
rebate → volume rebate programs, tier schedules, calculation methodology
territory → geographic definitions and restrictions
compliance → reporting requirements, audit rights, internal controls
general → term duration, termination, dispute resolution
payment → payment terms and schedules
product → product definitions and coverage
customer_segment → distributor/reseller appointment terms
These categories are what create the tab navigation on the rules page (the tabs you see like "Pricing & Rates 9", "Rebate Programs 10", etc.).
